What Is a Fake APK and How to Avoid It

An APK is an Android app installation file. Fake APKs pretend to be official apps but may steal OTPs, read notifications, abuse accessibility permissions, or redirect users to fraud pages.

How fake APKs spread

  • WhatsApp messages claiming a bank KYC update is required.
  • Fake electricity bill or courier tracking links.
  • Loan app ads promising instant money.
  • Investment groups sharing “special” trading apps.
  • Fake customer support pages asking users to install helper apps.

Red flags

Be cautious if an app asks for SMS, notification access, accessibility services, device admin access, or overlay permissions without a clear reason.

SecureVault approach

A fake APK detector should explain the risk in simple terms: where the app came from, what permissions it asks for, and what action the user should take next.
