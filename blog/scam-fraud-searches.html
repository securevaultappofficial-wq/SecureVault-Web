Scam and Fraud Searches Explained for Everyday Users

Scam searches are powerful for SecureVault because users often search only after receiving a suspicious message. Indian cyber advisories and fraud reports often highlight suspicious links, malicious APKs, fake KYC forms, OTP fraud, WhatsApp fraud, and online financial fraud.

  • WhatsApp scam link checker: A tool or guide that helps users judge if a WhatsApp link is fake, shortened, suspicious, or trying to steal data.
  • fake APK detector: A scanner or checklist that warns about dangerous Android app files shared outside trusted stores.
  • fake bank message checker: Helps users identify fake SMS or WhatsApp messages pretending to be from a bank.
  • UPI fraud complaint: Users search this after money loss. In India, call 1930 quickly and report on cybercrime.gov.in.
  • OTP scam: A scam where attackers trick users into sharing one-time passwords to access accounts or payments.
  • KYC scam message: A fake update message asking users to submit documents, click links, install APKs, or share banking details.
  • courier scam message: A fake delivery message claiming address failure, customs fee, or parcel hold to steal money or data.
  • digital arrest scam: Fraudsters pretend to be police or government officers and scare victims over video calls or calls.
  • investment scam WhatsApp group: Fake trading or investment groups show false profits to make users send money.
  • fake loan app: A risky app promising quick loans but abusing contacts, photos, harassment, and hidden charges.
  • fake job scam: Scammers promise jobs, ask for registration fees, or collect private documents.
  • phishing SMS checker: Helps users inspect SMS messages for fake domains, urgency, and data-stealing forms.
  • scam message detector: A feature that flags suspicious wording, risky links, fake authority, and payment pressure.
