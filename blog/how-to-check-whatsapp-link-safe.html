How to Check If a WhatsApp Link Is Safe

WhatsApp scam links often create urgency: blocked account, unpaid bill, KYC update, reward, courier issue, police warning, or investment profit. The goal is to make you tap before thinking.

Before opening a link

  • Check whether the domain name is spelled correctly.
  • Be careful with shortened URLs that hide the final destination.
  • Do not enter OTP, UPI PIN, card details, passwords, or Aadhaar details from a chat link.
  • Look for pressure words like urgent, final warning, account blocked, reward, or immediate arrest.
  • Search the company website manually instead of trusting the forwarded link.

Fake link examples

Common examples include fake bank messages, fake electricity bills, fake courier delivery links, investment groups, job application traps, and fake government forms.

Watch for APK downloads

If a link asks you to install an APK file, treat it as high risk. Fraud APKs can ask for SMS, notification, accessibility, or storage permissions to steal banking information.
